## Great People
Six types. Each can be **used immediately** (one-time, consumes the person) OR **settled** into a
city (permanent ongoing bonus). Each named person is tied to the tech whose discovery can spawn them.
(Acquisition channels elsewhere in the data: "Free Great Person" first-bonus on Monarchy & Invention;
civ bonuses â€” Greece/Rome "more Great People", America "begin with a Great Person"; Relic "School of
Confucius"; wonders.)

| Type | Immediate effect | Settle effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Great Artists/Thinkers** | Convert enemy cities | +50% culture production |
| **Great Builders** | Instantly complete any unit/building/wonder | Halve cost of all future buildings in the city |
| **Great Explorers/Industrialists** | Large one-time gold bonus | +50% gold production |
| **Great Humanitarians** | +1 population to ALL your cities | +50% population growth rate |
| **Great Leaders** | Upgrade all your units to veteran | New units get +3 exp (free upgrade if city has Barracks) |
| **Great Scientists** | Instantly finish current tech research | +50% science |

## Relics
Six artifacts, each found by exploring the map (reward to whoever locates it first).

| Relic | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Ark of the Covenant** | Builds a Temple in every city without one; cities that have Temples get Cathedrals instead |
| **Seven Cities of Gold** | Large gold bonus to treasury |
| **Angkor Wat** | Immediately builds a wonder in one of your cities |
| **Lost City of Atlantis** | Immediately completes research on several new technologies |
| **Knights Templar** | Gives you a powerful unit |
| **School of Confucius** | Grants the discoverer access to a slew of Great People |
