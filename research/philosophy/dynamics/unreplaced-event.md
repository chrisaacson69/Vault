# The Unreplaced Event — Why the West Still Runs on World War II
> Eighty years on, World War II still functions as the foundational event of the modern West — the origin point its institutions, its sense of "the modern era," and its definitions of ultimate good and evil all date from. Foundational frames don't decay on a timer; they get **replaced by a bigger event**, or they persist. WWII persists because it became *load-bearing substrate*, no successor event displaced it, it rides a uniquely *thick* carrier, and it is the *only available shared sacred for a pluralistic polity*. The frame also **mis-encoded its own founding lesson** — the mechanism of the crime was the *total state*, but the lesson got stored as *nationalism* — and much of today's political divide is, underneath, a disagreement about whether that 1945 frame is still correctly calibrated. (The apocalyptic reading — "the myth is killing the civilization" — is a separate, weaker claim; see the drain at the end.)

## The descriptive core (this part is not fringe)

A *foundation myth* — in the analytic, not the pejorative, sense — performs three functions: it (1) explains the origin and structure of the present world, (2) fixes the definitions of ultimate good and evil from which a society's values are derived, and (3) names what is held sacred and may not be mocked. The claim is that for the modern West, **WWII fills all three**: we live in the "post-war world" (origin), the ultimate evil is Nazism and the ultimate good is opposing it (good/evil → the values of anti-racism, equality, pluralism), and the Holocaust occupies the place of the sacred.

This is close to a serious literature, not an internet novelty:
- **Robert Bellah**, "Civil Religion in America" (1967) — modern secular states still run a sacred-civic layer.
- **Peter Novick**, *The Holocaust in American Life* (1999), and **Tony Judt**, *Postwar* (2005) — "Holocaust recognition is our contemporary European entry ticket."
- **Aleida Assmann / Peter Reichel** — the explicit concept of a **"negative memory" / negative founding myth** in German identity, which the popular version is essentially reinventing.

So the descriptive observation — *WWII/Holocaust as the post-war West's civil religion, structured around a pole of avoidance* — is defensible and worth taking seriously independent of any politics attached to it.

## Why a frame persists 80 years

Frames are replaced, not aged out. Four mechanisms keep this one live:

1. **It became load-bearing substrate.** By the [symmetry-breaking](./symmetry-breaking.md) propagation story, once enough structure leans on a frozen asymmetry, reversing it costs more than the system can pay. The UN, the human-rights regime, the legitimacy of European integration, and the post-war delegitimation of empire all date from and rest on the WWII frame. It persists not because the memory is fresh but because it is now *foundation*.
2. **No successor event.** A bigger foundational event overwrites the prior one. The Cold War ended without a hot climax (1991 had no V-E Day); 9/11 auditioned and lacked the scale and moral clarity. WWII persists by *default* — nothing displaced it.
3. **A uniquely thick carrier.** Curricula, museums, mandatory remembrance, the Holocaust-studies field, and an unkillable film canon transmit the frame with an institutional density few myths ever achieve. (Godwin's Law — that any online argument eventually reaches a Hitler comparison — is the folk acknowledgment that the frame is the West's default maximal moral reference.)
4. **It is the equilibrium sacred of a *pluralistic* polity.** "Never again genocide" is the **minimum viable shared sacred** for a society that agrees on little else. A *particularist* positive myth ("we, this people, are glorious") cannot be the shared sacred of a polity containing many peoples — it excludes by construction. Once a society is plural (from empire, trade, migration — whatever material cause), the only available shared sacred is a *thin, universal, avoidance-shaped* one. On this reading the negative-universal frame is not a decadent choice; it is the **shadow of pluralism**, which is itself downstream of real material history.

## The mis-encoding — *total state*, stored as *nationalism*

This is the load-bearing analytical claim, and it is testable rather than mythic. The frame encodes the lesson of the catastrophe as **"ethnic nationalism leads to genocide."** But genocide is poorly predicted by nationalism:

- **China** is ~92% Han — about as ethnically consolidated as a great power gets — and its campaign against the Uyghurs is driven by *religious/separatist suppression by a totalizing state*, the opposite of nationalist expansion.
- **The Khmer Rouge** murdered fellow Khmers — a *class/ideological* purge with zero ethnonational content.
- **The Holodomor, the Great Leap, the Gulag** — *class/political* enemy-categories.

Nationalism is therefore **neither necessary nor sufficient** for genocide. The actual common denominator is **totalizing, unchecked state power × a dehumanized enemy-category** — and the category can be ethnic, religious, class, or political. The Nazi crime ran on *Gleichschaltung* (the total coordination of state and society) at least as much as on ethnic ideology; the ethnicity was the *enemy-category*, the total state was the *engine*.

So the founding lesson got stored one level too shallow. The correct prophylactic against the catastrophe is **"don't let power become total"** — keep it divided, federated, checked — which is exactly the conclusion the [Gödel-governance problem](./the-godel-governance-problem.md) and [christian-advantage's decentralization verdict](./christian-structural-advantage.md) reach by other routes, and which the [optimal-scale law](../../economics/theory-of-the-firm.md) explains (a monopoly is a collective past its optimal size whose outward pressure has been suppressed). The stored lesson — "don't be nationalist" — is a *symptom-correlate*, not the cause. Worse, the institutions built to prevent "another Hitler" tend to *centralize* power, so the immune response can quietly build more of the real pathogen (total coordination) while attacking a non-pathogen (ordinary national solidarity). That miscalibration — antibodies that can no longer tell the pathogen from healthy tissue — is the structural core of what the popular version calls the West's inability to "take its own side."

## Why this drives the political divide

The divide is, underneath, a disagreement about **whether the 1945 frame is still correctly calibrated** — and both sides argue *from inside it*:

- One side experiences the frame as settled sacred truth: the anti-fascist / anti-racist / pluralist order is the hard-won lesson, and reopening it is dangerous.
- The other experiences it as a *stale, misaimed, increasingly self-indicting* frame whose founding instruction (avoid the total state) has been overwritten by a category error (avoid the nation), and which therefore should no longer be load-bearing.

Neither side is outside the myth; they disagree about its calibration and its expiry. Naming that is more useful than either side's self-description, because it explains why the argument is so heated and so unresolvable — it is a fight over a *sacred*, conducted by people who share the same sacred.

## History rhymes — the grudge as a maintained structure

The same dynamics explain why such frames recur across civilizations. A grudge is a **frozen asymmetry kept *dissipatively* alive**: an original injury freezes into identity, accumulates load until release is prohibitive, and then requires *active maintenance* (commemoration, curricula, ritual) to persist. Two consequences:

- **"Endless war" is institutionalized non-termination.** Korea ended in *armistice, never a treaty*; China's "Century of Humiliation" is an explicitly open-ended narrative. The grudge encodes a refusal to accept the equilibrium — the [N≥3 termination problem](../../gaming/n3-termination-and-randomization.md) at civilizational scale.
- **The discriminating variable is present political utility, not injury severity.** Grudges persist where a *current* power benefits from maintaining them (regime legitimation, mobilization), and fade where the retelling is starved.

Two refinements matter, because the popular version gets both backwards. First, the operative axis among negative founding frames is **self-indicting vs. other-indicting**: China's humiliation myth points *outward* (foreigners did this → rise) and mobilizes; a guilt frame points *inward* and demobilizes — and an identity built on *opposing oppression* (the Anglo "stood against tyranny" self-image) will, once external enemies deplete, turn on its own past for fuel. Second, **grudges end by payoff-shift, not by forming a collective**: parties reconcile when each independently calculates that cooperation beats feuding (US–Japan, US–Germany became *alliances of interest*, never a shared identity). Franco-German reconciliation used the ECSC/EU as a *commitment device after* the payoff had already shifted — so a self-interested nation can absolutely make peace; collectivization is one costly, durable path, not the mechanism.

## The drain — what to keep and what to discard

The structural thesis above is testable and survives. The popular version usually adds an **apocalyptic monocausal** layer — "the negative myth is poisoning the tree of life; the civilization is dying of it" — and *that* layer fails the same [confound wall](./christian-structural-advantage.md) the vault built for "Christianity → flourishing":

- Decline (if real) is overdetermined — fertility is sub-replacement across *every* high-income society regardless of founding myth, and East Asia (no WWII-guilt frame; Korea ~0.7, Japan ~1.2) declines *fastest*. If a guilt-frame were the load-bearing cause, the society without one should be healthiest. It is not.
- The "civilizational self-image has collapsed" explanandum is partly real (the WVS "willing to fight for your country" gap) but confounded with *cosmopolitan substitution*, inflated by the perennial declinist genre, and concentrated in an *elite* class rather than whole populations.
- Self-criticism may be a *luxury of security*, not a symptom of death — only a safe, dominant civilization can afford sustained self-indictment.

And the apocalyptic version is **performatively self-undermining**: "our civilization is dying of its founding wound" is *itself* an origin-as-wound, villain-at-the-center, sacred-obscenity narrative — a negative foundation myth about negative foundation myths. So: **keep** the structural claim (a foundation frame can become load-bearing, mis-encode its own lesson, and persist past the conditions that justified it); **discard** the prophecy.

## Open question

The one researchable residue: controlling for development level, do **self-indicting** founding frames, carried by **thin broadcast** media, in an **elite** class, measurably depress national self-efficacy relative to other-indicting or generative frames? That is hard but honest — and it is a far smaller, far more defensible claim than the one the popular telling makes.
