# The Map of Philosophy — The Textbook Four Domains vs. the Vault's Four Trunks
> The popular map of philosophy is three questions plus a tool: *what exists* (metaphysics), *what can we know* (epistemology), *what should we do* (ethics), with logic as the manipulator. That map is correct and useful — and it's exactly what the vault's [Four Trunks](./the-four-trunks.md) become once you stop describing the questions and commit to mechanisms. The interesting part is where the two maps fail to line up.

## The Textbook Map

The video's framing is the standard one, and it's a good one. Philosophy mirrors the science hierarchy — math → physics → chemistry → biology → psychology, each emerging when the layer below gets complicated enough — with the same feature that *the borders are deliberately blurry* (where does chemistry stop and biology start?). Philosophy reduces to **three questions plus one tool**:

| Question | Domain | Subfields tucked under it |
|---|---|---|
| What exists? | **Metaphysics** | philosophy of mind, time, space, causation |
| What can we know? | **Epistemology** | justification, perception, the limits of knowledge |
| What should we do? | **Ethics** | aesthetics, political philosophy, value theory |
| *(the tool, not a question)* | **Logic** | validity vs. truth, formal logic, the fallacies |

This is the right entry-point. You don't need an encyclopedic knowledge of dead Greeks; you need to know which of the three questions a given idea is answering, and that logic is how you move between them.

## The Bridge to the Four Trunks

The vault's [Four Trunks](./the-four-trunks.md) are the same questions after they've been forced to declare a **mechanism and a stance**. The textbook map describes the questions; the trunks answer them.

| Textbook domain | Vault trunk | What changes when you commit |
|---|---|---|
| What exists? | **Trunk 1 — Normative Ontology** (*constitutive realism*) | "What's real" gets a non-arbitrary answer for norms: facts entailed by the structure of the relata. |
| What can we know? | **Trunk 2 — Verification Epistemology** (*constitutive convergence*) | "How we know" gets a method: independent convergence under reality-pressure. |
| *(folded into "what exists")* | **Trunk 3 — Emergence Metaphysics** | Split out: *how* the real arises from organized matter is a different question from *what* is real. |
| What should we do? | **Trunk 1** (norms) + **Trunk 4** (dynamics) | Ethics splits — the *content* of norms is Trunk 1; their *persistence and drift over time* is Trunk 4. |
| Logic (the tool) | **Trunk 3** anchor (logic-and-math) | Logic is the tool *and* a domain with real structure ([structural realism](./logic-and-math/emergence-and-convergence.md)). |

## Where the Two Maps Diverge

The bridge is not one-to-one, and the gaps are the load-bearing part.

**1. The video folds emergence into "what exists." The vault splits it out (Trunk 3).** Asking *what is real* and asking *how the real arises from the material substrate* are different questions. The video treats "what is consciousness?" as a metaphysics question alongside "what is matter?" The vault separates them: matter is ontology (Trunk 1's domain), but consciousness-from-neurons, arithmetic-from-transistors, logic-from-cognition is an **emergence** question (Trunk 3) — real immaterial patterns arising from organized matter. The textbook map has no slot for this distinction.

**2. The video has no dynamics axis at all (Trunk 4 is missing).** The standard map is *static* — it asks what is, what's knowable, what's right, but never *how real structured systems behave over time*. The vault's fourth trunk — every concentration of power generates counter-forces, every governing system hits a [Gödel-shaped wall](./dynamics/the-godel-governance-problem.md), every civilization follows recognizable arcs — has no home on the textbook map. This is the single biggest thing the popular map omits, and it's where much of the vault's distinctive work lives.

**3. The video stops at "metaphysics is inscrutable." The vault doesn't shrug.** The video's honest conclusion is that the foundations are "fundamentally a bit inscrutable, which is why science and religions and individual people all have completely different answers." True as far as it goes — but it plants its flag at the shrug. The vault plants it two moves further: even with unjustifiable foundations, you can still (a) use the **[performative escape](./epistemology/the-regress-problem.md)** for the axioms that are constitutive of reasoning itself, and (b) **evaluate the structures** built on any foundation by convergence under reality-pressure (Trunk 2). "Inscrutable foundation" does not entail "all answers equal." See [The Regress Problem](./epistemology/the-regress-problem.md).

> **The author is a latent structuralist.** Two tells betray it. First, he accepts the brute-fact horn *calmly* — "sometimes you just get to a point where things *are*, there's nothing further to look into." That isn't a shrug at failure; it's a structuralist treating the bottom of the why-chain as a *floor*, not a wound — the same posture as the vault's "structure is the truth-maker." Second, he lays out all three horns and stops, never reaching for the fourth move (**performative contradiction**), which is exactly the move that also [bridges the is-ought gap](./morality/is-ought-and-performative-grounding.md): the questioner is already standing on the side they're asking about. He runs the classical breakdown but keeps brushing the vault's answer without the vocabulary to name it — one concept short of the whole exit. The near-miss is itself worth logging as a (weak, n=1) **Trunk-2 convergence datum**: an independent popularizer with no contact with this framework arriving comfortably at the brute-fact floor is a non-communicating agent landing on the same shape under reality-pressure.

The overview carries several arguments worth more than their runtime. Each already has, or now gets, a vault home:

The vault adds the fourth-kind-of-justification escape. |

| **Ethics rests on implicit metaphysics** — abortion ↔ consciousness; every political philosophy smuggles a metaphysics | [Four Trunks joints](./the-four-trunks.md#how-the-trunks-connect) | Agrees. | **Aesthetics as transmitted convention** — courthouse columns: Greek → Roman → Renaissance, "no one person decided" | Trunk 2 (convergence) / spontaneous order | Agrees. "No one decided" is the signature of an emergent convention, not a designed one — the same shape as convergence. |

## The One-Line Takeaway

The textbook map is the vault's map with two axes collapsed: emergence folded into ontology, and dynamics dropped entirely. Re-add those two and you have the Four Trunks. The video's real philosophical contribution — the regress trichotomy — is also the vault's oldest unfinished business, which is why it now gets [its own canonical page](./epistemology/the-regress-problem.md).
