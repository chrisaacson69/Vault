# Force Doctrine — The Theory
> Andrew Wilson's "rights don't exist, only force" is a constitutive realism that took two wrong exits. Walk its own path honestly and it lands on structure, not God-by-the-gun. The man is more right than his framework.

**Links:** [Rights Don't Exist, Only Force (the early statement)](../../debates/wilson-rights-dont-exist-only-force.md), [Force Doctrine & Pragmatism](../../debates/wilson-force-doctrine-pragmatism.md), [Wilson on Triggernometry](../../debates/wilson-triggernometry-christian-nationalism.md), [The Constitutive Middle](./constitutive-middle-dialectics.md), [Scope Confusion](./scope-confusion.md)

---

This is the doctrinal spin-off the two Wilson debate clips sparked — the *argument*, with the debates as evidence. The thesis: **Force Doctrine is mostly right, and where it's wrong it's wrong in two locatable places.** Correct those two and Wilson's framework converges on the vault's structural realism.

## What Force Doctrine claims

Stated cleanly (from the [earliest full statement](../../debates/wilson-rights-dont-exist-only-force.md)):

1. **Ontological deflation** — rights are intangible ("can you taste it, touch it, smell it?"), therefore conceptual, therefore they "don't exist."
2. **Definition** — a right is "an entitlement absent a duty"; duties, by contrast, are real (from God).
3. **Reduction to force** — to assert a right is to demand that force uphold it; "at the end of every law is a gun." So rights-talk *is* force-talk.
4. **Parity** — the secularist admits his rights are made up yet enforces them with force; so he has "no leg to stand on" to deny the Christian the same move. "Your own form of Sharia law."
5. **Tiebreaker** — pressed on whether his force is then equally arbitrary, Wilson gestures at "the truth claim of who's actually in the right," and stops.

## The true core — what sticks

**Force-as-action makes reality.** At the deepest level this is true and even praxeological: to change anything you must *act*, and action is causation — reality is forces acting on forces; an intention that never propagates to action moves nothing. ("Might makes *reality*," not "might makes *right*" — the descriptive truth the NAP-libertarians wrongly thought they had to *deny* to protect their principle. They didn't; you grant it for free, because it's normatively inert.)

**The rights/duties distinction is real — and it is the boundary of force's reality-making power.** This is Wilson's best idea, and his two best ideas are secretly one:
- **Rights = the compelled, external layer** — what we *force* on people. Here "reduces to force" is *true by definition*: a legal right just is a claim the state backs with a gun.
- **Duties = the voluntary, internal layer** — how people *ought to interact*, uncompelled. Here force is **constitutively impotent**: coerced virtue isn't virtue, forced charity isn't charity, compelled assent isn't belief. Force can counterfeit the behavior, never the reality, because the reality is partly constituted by its un-coerced origin.

So "force makes reality" is true of the rights-layer and **false of the duty-layer** — and the duty-layer holds the realities that matter most. Wilson concedes this against himself in round 1 ("I don't think you can enforce someone to believe something") and walks past it. Worse for him: *Christianity* is the tradition most committed to the point — God wants the **free** assent of the creature; a coerced conversion is worthless. His own anthropology of free will refutes the universal reduction.

**The "racketing" diagnosis is correct.** The culture has hypertrophied rights (claims on others, state-enforced) and let duties (what I owe, uncompelled) atrophy. This is a real, structural decay — and self-reinforcing: as the duty-fabric dissolves, coercion becomes the *only* remaining mechanism for getting people to behave. **Duty-atrophy causes force-hypertrophy.**

**The present is a contest, and quietism is wrong.** "Structure wins eventually" is a long-run claim, not a license to sit above the fight. Someone has to hold the line now. Wilson's willingness to fight as hard as his opponents is the *correct* response to the dynamic — his praxis is sound.

## What breaks — four bad inferences off a correct premise

1. **immaterial → unreal.** Category error. The touch/taste/smell test measures *materiality*, not reality; it deletes numbers, logic, and *his own God and duties* alongside rights. And the criterion ("only the sensible exists") is itself an insensible proposition — self-refuting. (Also: writing a right down materializes a *record* of it, not the right; and you never touch "the phone itself" either — both are known through representations. The honest axis is material/immaterial, not real/unreal.)

2. **constructed → arbitrary.** "Made up" splits into three axes Wilson collapses: *constructed-in-origin* (true of rights), *arbitrary* (false), *causally inert* (false). Chess is made up but checkmate isn't up for grabs; money is made up but buys bread. Mind-dependent in origin ≠ arbitrary in content ≠ unreal in effect. (This is the vault's [structure thesis](./constitutive-middle-dialectics.md); Wilson can concede it and still fire — which is why the ontology was never the crux.)

3. **enforced-by-force → reduces-to-force.** The load-bearing equivocation. "Force" carries two senses: **force-as-action/causation** (everything that impacts reality) and **force-as-coercion** (men with machine guns). The breadth that makes "force makes reality" *true* (action-in-general) is exactly the breadth that makes "rights reduce to force" *useless* — because then a vote, a sermon, a contract, and a handshake are all force too, and the reduction stops discriminating the gun from the ballot. There is no setting where the claim is both true and does his work: read as *action* it's true and inert; read as *coercion* it's false (most order runs on internalized norm, not the gun — Hume: "on opinion only is government founded"; the cop-per-citizen point). Wilson **borrows the universal truth of force-as-action and spends it as the menace of force-as-coercion.** Praxeologically: action is the genus, coercion one species; he collapses the genus into the species and pockets the difference.

4. **force-symmetry → mutual uncriticizability.** Force enforces an *is*; it cannot manufacture an *ought*. "X has the most guns" never yields "X *should* rule." So steps 1–4 are an anti-realist **leveling move** (we're both made-up force-wielders, a *draw*), and to convert the draw into a win Wilson needs step 5 — *Christianity is true* — which (i) he never argues and (ii) contradicts the anti-realism he just used. He can't hold "we're symmetric, you can't criticize me" *and* "but mine is true" in the same breath. The whole apparatus is a machine for laundering an *is* into an *ought*, running only because no one makes him state the *ought* aloud. (Same hole as [Wilson vs Objectivism](../../debates/wilson-objectivism-stance-dependence.md): he exploits a regress while standing in it.)

**The unifying diagnosis:** every move is the *selective application of a universal solvent*. Tangibility, made-up-ness, force-reduction — each dissolves *everything* if applied evenly (his God, his duties, the gun/vote distinction). He wins only by acid-washing the opponent's side of the table and wiping his own clean.

## The duty error and the Euthyphro correction

Wilson's deepest claim — *only God creates duties, and they are absolutely right* — bundles a truth and an error:
- **(i) Duties are real and, in the limit, absolute.** ✔ Keep it.
- **(ii) Their absoluteness is divine fiat.** ✗ Drop it.

Euthyphro forks (ii): either God *could have* willed otherwise (murder = good), making duties **arbitrary at the divine level** — God's preferences backed by ultimate force, i.e. *divine might-makes-right*, the very arbitrariness he accused the secularist of, relocated one floor up — or God commands them *because* they already bind given what a person is, in which case **the truth-maker is the structure and God is the Author of the natures, not the author of the duties** (the [constitutive middle](./constitutive-middle-dialectics.md): God = Author, not truth-maker).

This correction is *more* Christian than Wilson, not less: Aquinas already took the second horn — murder is wrong because of what rational creatures *are* (their telos), which God authored. **Natural law is constitutive realism with a theistic Author bolted on.** Wilson's crude will-based grounding isn't even his own tradition's strongest. So we keep "duties are real and absolute" and replace "because decreed" with "because **structural**." This is [wisdom-compression](../../debates/trent-horn-vs-notsoerudite-christian-nationalism.md), not a dunk: "God commands it" is a lossy-but-functional carrier of "it's constitutive of what you are." Decompress; don't discard.

## The reconstruction — what the corrected path builds

Once force-as-action is the true genus, the live question is no longer "is it force?" (everything is) but **what selects and channels among exercises of force.**

- **Rights/laws/constitutions are the governance of force**, not its denial or its mask: devices that make the settling of contested claims *predictable, general, pre-committed, revisable* instead of arbitrary. A written, general, revisable right is a commitment device — it changes what the force *does* (bounded vs. arbitrary), which is the entire content of constitutionalism that the reduction erases.
- **Duties are the force-immune layer** that must be *cultivated*, not compelled — and a healthy order rests mostly on it, with coercion as the residual backstop for defectors.
- **Structure selects who holds the force across rounds.** Force is the proximate cause of each battle; structural fitness (coherence, reproduction, coordination, fit-to-human-nature) is the fitness function that decides, over many rounds, who *gets* to hold the force — a structurally-wrong ideology loses its grip on the material/demographic base the gun runs on. Force wins the round; structure wins the war. **Guard:** structural fitness must be *independently specifiable* in advance, or "structure wins" collapses into vacuous historicism ("whoever won was fit"). The arrow is fit → durable might, never might → right.

## What the doctrine is *for* — leveling an asymmetric exclusion

Force Doctrine's *truth* and its *function* are different questions. In Wilson's hands it isn't primarily a metaphysics — it's a counter-move to an asymmetric game, and the grievance under it is real.

**The asymmetry he's reacting to.** The secular-liberal frame treats its own premises as the neutral default and religious premises as sectarian intrusions to be bracketed — Rawlsian public reason, operationalized as *"you can't legislate your religion."* But progressive liberalism is itself a contestable comprehensive doctrine being legislated; it just doesn't count itself as one. So the Christian faces a three-part move: his values are (a) denied existence, (b) ruled inadmissible for policy, while (c) the other side enforces its own anyway. That's not disagreement — it's a demand for *second-class epistemic citizenship*, the same asymmetric "Christians must justify, seculars need not" standard the [Trent Horn / Kyla debate](../../debates/trent-horn-vs-notsoerudite-christian-nationalism.md) dissects.

**Valid as defense, invalid as offense.** As a *defensive* solvent, force-doctrine works: parity-of-reasoning legitimately revokes the exclusion — you cannot bar someone by a standard you exempt yourself from. But revoking the privilege returns you to a *symmetric* contest; it does not hand you the win. Once the field is level ("we're both enforcing made-up values"), the question *"whose values should govern?"* is **reopened, not answered** — and that is the exact question the reduction was built to skip. Wilson uses the leveler to *avoid* the contest, when all leveling does is force you to finally have it.

**The leveler is the door, not the room** — and his best material is *inside* the room he won't enter. The duty-layer is real, his tradition is a structural reservoir, and on *content* he could win many exchanges. But entering means arguing content, which means conceding content is *judgeable*, which contradicts the reduction he used to open the door. His own solvent strands his strongest case outside: he wins the right to be in the fight, then forfeits it by denying there's anything to fight about but firepower.

**The danger:** if the leveler hands off to *force* rather than to *structure*, he hasn't defended his beliefs — he's reinvented might-makes-right, the thing he disclaims ("I'm not sure it's not the best way to handle it," re: the machine guns). The level field is where *structure* is supposed to adjudicate, not where the bigger gun wins.

**The generous read is sequencing.** In a real fight, you may have to break the asymmetric exclusion *before* you can have the content argument at all: step 1, revoke the privilege (force-doctrine); step 2, win on structure. Wilson does step 1 loudly and necessarily — his failure is mistaking the opening for the whole game. The answer to him isn't "stop fighting"; it's *"you're right to refuse the asymmetry — now walk through the door you opened, because the room is your home turf."*

## The thesis

> **Wilson is a constitutive realist who made two mislocations.** He read the immaterial as *unreal* (it is real-as-structure), and he attributed *structure's* work to *God's will* (structure is the truth-maker; God, if any, is the Author of the natures). Correct those two and walk his own path honestly — rights vs duties, the duty-layer is real and decaying, the present is a contest settled through force — and you arrive not at divine-command Christian nationalism but at **structural realism.** A fellow traveler who took one wrong exit and blamed God for what structure does.

And the charitable coda: Wilson is a man of his time watching duty collapse, drawing on a religion that *is* a time-tested reservoir of structural content, and willing to fight for it. His **praxis is sounder than his theory** — when something works there's structural fit to recover even when the stated reason is wrong. His "losing battle" isn't losing if the duty-layer is genuinely structural (it is; rights-only orders are terminal, not stable). What he calls "the righteous will prevail," the vault calls "structure wins." Same bet, different label.

## Toolkit

Portable moves against the reduction (the debaters had none of these loaded):

- **Split the two forces.** Make him pick: force-as-action (true, inert — gives him nothing) or force-as-coercion (substantive, but then the reduction is false). No reading is both true and useful.
- **Enforcement ≠ justification.** "How a norm is backed" is a different question from "whether it's justified." He's answered the first and called it the second.
- **Made-up ≠ arbitrary ≠ unreal.** Three axes; he needs them welded; chess and money pry them apart.
- **A tu quoque is a draw.** "Suppose I'm as ungrounded as you say — that gives *you* nothing. Where's your tiebreaker?" Then make him spend step 5 (Christian *truth*) under the anti-realism that forbids it.
- **Concede the leveler, then collect.** When he deploys parity as a permission slip: "Agreed — neither of us gets a neutral exemption. So we're back to arguing whose values are structurally better. Let's — that's the argument your reduction was built to skip." Accept the door; make him enter the room.
- **Euthyphro the duties.** Divine fiat relocates arbitrariness to God; the escape (nature, not will) *is* the structural view. Aquinas already conceded it.
- **Refuse the tangibility test aloud** — it deletes his God and duties, and it can't pass itself.

## Open Questions

1. **Does the reduction have *any* teeth once both forces are named?** If "force" must be disambiguated into action (inert) or coercion (false), is there a third reading that's both true and load-bearing — or is the doctrine's entire efficacy the equivocation?
2. **How independently specifiable is structural fitness, really?** The historicism guard demands we read fitness off the relata *in advance*. How far can we actually do that before it becomes retrodiction?
3. **Is the duty-layer recoverable by design, or only by collapse-and-reselection?** If duty-atrophy is terminal, do healthy duty-fabrics get *rebuilt* deliberately, or only *re-selected* after the rights-only order fails?
