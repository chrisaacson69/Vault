# Morality — Open Problems

## 1. The Filter Problem

Cross-cultural convergence includes bad patterns too (slavery, patriarchy, out-group violence). If convergence validates "don't steal," does it validate "subjugate your enemies"? Directionality (error-correction over time) is the proposed filter, but the timescale can be centuries. What does a person living inside an uncorrected error do?

## 2. Naming an Absolute Moral Truth

"Murder is wrong" begs the question (murder = wrongful killing by definition). Theistic "God is good" pushes the is-ought gap to the deity. Can a non-theological absolute be identified?

The slavery case is instructive: slavery requires a victim with agency (a mind), so the moral claim is mind-dependent by construction. But given agency, no coherent agent can defend slavery without undermining their own agency (performative contradiction). So is "slavery is wrong" objective? It depends on whether "objective" means "mind-independent" (no — it requires minds) or "stance-independent given minds" (yes — no agent can coherently deny it). The standard objective/subjective dichotomy may be the wrong frame entirely. See #5 below.

The framework may not need an absolute if probabilistic heuristics evaluated by aggregate outcomes are sufficient.

## 3. The Timescale Problem

Immoral strategies can dominate for generations. Error correction is a civilizational process, not an individual one. How does an individual navigate when the lagging indicators haven't caught up yet?

## 4. The Dual-Path Structure

The vault accepts two paths to moral truths: empirical convergence and performative grounding. These are NOT independent parallel arguments — the vault's position is that empirics come first, and given enough empirical data, the logical structure reveals itself. Performative contradiction is the *formalization* of what the empirics already showed, not a separate path.

The chain: experiment -> convergence -> logical explanation in hindsight -> formalization -> forward prediction.

Example: "slavery is wrong" was discovered empirically across civilizations over millennia; the performative contradiction argument is the logical skeleton extracted from that data. Similarly, religious moral prohibitions (e.g., contraception) often encode logical structures discoverable only on civilizational timescales — cached consequentialism from a dataset too large for any individual to observe.

The weakness that remains: "might makes reality" keeps reasserting itself because the brute who acts without arguing faces no performative contradiction, and empirical convergence operates on centuries, not lifetimes. (Clarified 2026-04-05.)

## ~~5. The Objective/Subjective Paradox~~ — RESOLVED

**Resolution:** The paradox dissolves because "objective" and "subjective" each conflate two independent axes: (1) mind-dependent vs mind-independent, and (2) constitutive vs elective. Split the axes and both paths are correct — morality IS mind-dependent (path A, axis 1) AND constitutive/non-arbitrary (path B, axis 2). There's no paradox; the two paths answer different questions.

The constitutive/elective distinction replaces the broken objective/subjective vocabulary. Full argument: [The Constitutive/Elective Distinction](./constitutive-elective.md). (Resolved 2026-04-08 via [Noerr vs Terry X debate review](../../debates/noerr-teryx-subjective-objective-morality.md).)

## 6. Mind/Brain Emergence and the Physicalism Boundary

The vault holds that mind is emergent from brain — physical but not reducible to physics ([Emergence and Convergence](../logic-and-math/emergence-and-convergence.md)).

This dissolves a false dichotomy that both physicalists and dualists rely on:
- If mind IS physical (emergent), you can't dismiss moral claims as "non-physical"
- But you also can't reduce moral claims to physics, because the emergent layer has properties not present at the substrate level
- This undermines both "morality is a law of physics" (TJump) and "morality is non-physical, therefore God" (theists)

The dichotomy between physical and non-physical may be as false as the objective/subjective one in #5. Both debates assume a clean boundary that emergence dissolves. (Surfaced 2026-04-05 via TJump panel debate.)
