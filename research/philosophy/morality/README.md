## The framework in three lines

- **Morality requires agents** — physical reality, consciousness, agency (the [is-ought bridge](./is-ought-and-performative-grounding.md) is performative, not logical).
- **Given agents, the norms are constitutive** — not arbitrary, entailed by the structure of agent-interaction. See [Constitutive/Elective Distinction](./constitutive-elective.md).
- **Norms are scope-bounded, not universal** — core norms are structurally required; marginal norms are elective. See [Scope Confusion](./scope-confusion.md).

## Three overlapping circles

- **Morality** — right and wrong at individual conscience
- **Ethics** — group norms
- **Law** — codified ethics with enforcement

These overlap, they don't nest. Confusing the circles produces most political disagreement — see [scope-confusion](./scope-confusion.md).

## Two layers of good and bad

Action (praxeology) and morality aren't identical. Praxeological success = "did you achieve your purpose." Moral evaluation = "does your purpose, when pursued, sustain or degrade the conditions under which agents can function." Successful theft is praxeologically good and constitutively bad. Full treatment: [Constitutive/Elective Distinction](./constitutive-elective.md).
