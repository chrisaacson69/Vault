# HoP Episode Writeup Instructions

## Process

Each episode writeup follows a two-pass process:

### Pass 1: Fresh Analysis (Independent)
- Summarize what Peikoff actually argues in the lecture
- Evaluate his reasoning on its own terms
- Note strengths and weaknesses of the presented arguments
- Reference ONLY previous episodes in this series for context
- Do NOT reference the Vault's philosophy, frameworks, or positions
- Do NOT connect to morality, TAG, emergence, agency, or other Vault topics
- Approach the material as if encountering these ideas for the first time
- The goal is to understand Peikoff's narrative on its own terms

### Pass 2: Vault Connections (Separate, Later)
- After Pass 1 is complete and reviewed, add connections to Vault pages
- This is where cross-links, agreements, disagreements, and analysis go
- Clearly marked as a separate section ("Vault Connections" or similar)
- This pass can happen in a different session

## Key Principle
The value of this series is approaching the history of philosophy on its own terms FIRST, then seeing how it connects to the Vault's independent work. If we start from the Vault and work backward, we just confirm what we already believe. If we start fresh and find convergence, that's real evidence.
