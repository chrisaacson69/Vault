## About the Course

Leonard Peikoff's "History of Western Philosophy" is a 50-lecture survey covering ~2,400 years — from the pre-Socratics (6th century BC) through Kant (18th century). Peikoff is an Objectivist and presents the material through that lens, but the course's real value is how he draws the threads together: each philosopher responding to the last, errors compounding across centuries, and the consequences showing up in culture, politics, and art.

The course divides into:
- **Ancient** (6th BC – 6th AD): Pre-Socratics, Socrates/Plato, Aristotle, post-Aristotelians
- **Medieval** (4th–15th AD): Augustinian era (Plato + Christianity), Thomistic era (Aristotle + Christianity)
- **Modern** (15th–18th): Rationalists (Descartes, Spinoza, Leibniz), Empiricists (Locke, Berkeley, Hume), Kant

## Purpose of This Section

These pages are not primarily about cross-linking into the vault's existing framework. The goal is to:
1. **Track the course** — summarize what Peikoff actually argues in each lecture
2. **Evaluate the chain** — does his narrative of ideas-causing-ideas hold up? Has history been kind to these philosophers or vindicated them?
3. **Note where he's strongest and weakest** — the Objectivist lens sharpens some things and distorts others
