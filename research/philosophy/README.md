# Philosophy
> First-principles exploration of foundational philosophical questions.

**Links:** [The Four Trunks](./the-four-trunks.md), [Economics](../economics/README.md), [The Cyborg Model](../cyborg-model.md)

## Overall Structure

The vault's philosophy is organized as [four interlocking trunks](./the-four-trunks.md): normative ontology (*constitutive realism*), verification epistemology (*constitutive convergence*), emergence metaphysics (unnamed), and structural dynamics (unnamed). Each trunk does distinct work; no single name covers all four. The four-trunks page is the canonical organizing document for how the vault's philosophical commitments fit together.

## Branches

- [Morality](./morality/README.md) — what is good, what is right, and why
- [Logic and Mathematics](./logic-and-math/README.md) — structural realism; formalisms as maps, not territory
- [Epistemology](./epistemology/README.md) — (stub) how we know what we know; Objectivist engagement; non-human cognition

- [Measurement, Causality, and Free Will](./metaphysics/measurement-causality.md) — measurement as the basis of causality; consciousness as simulation; physical grounding of free will
- [Civilizational Cycles](./dynamics/civilizational-cycles/README.md) — theories of rise and fall; Ibn Khaldun, Spengler, Toynbee, Turchin, Tainter, Hayek, and their connections to economics and the voluntary-to-coercion drift
- [Opposing Forces](./dynamics/opposing-forces.md) — why every concentration of power generates counter-forces; thermodynamic, computational, game-theoretic, evolutionary, and agential explanations
- [The Fallacy Fallacy](./tangents/the-fallacy-fallacy.md) — why naming a fallacy isn't a rebuttal; the "why" requirement; misapplied fallacy labels; strange loops in fallacy hunting

## Notes

Philosophy here follows the same approach as the economics research: build from bedrock concepts upward, test against real cases, and connect back to the practical questions (AI alignment, agent design, human/AI collaboration).
