# The Four Trunks — The Structure of the Vault's Philosophy
> The vault's philosophy is not a single thesis with one name. It's four interlocking commitments, each doing distinct work. Two of the four have working names (constitutive realism, constitutive convergence); two are still unnamed. The umbrella name is deferred until the trunks are each named, because it has to be what the four names point at together.

**Links:** [Philosophy](./README.md), [Constitutive/Elective Distinction](./morality/constitutive-elective.md), [Stance-Independence Retreat](./epistemology/stance-independence-retreat.md), [Relational Objectivity](./epistemology/relational-objectivity.md), [The Birthmark](./epistemology/the-birthmark.md), [Emergence, Convergence, and the Strange Loop](./logic-and-math/emergence-and-convergence.md), [Opposing Forces](./dynamics/opposing-forces.md), [Measurement, Causality, and Free Will](./metaphysics/measurement-causality.md), [The Gödel Governance Problem](./dynamics/the-godel-governance-problem.md)

## Why Four Trunks

The vault's pages answer four different kinds of question and each kind requires a different claim:

- **What is real, and why?** (ontology)
- **How do we know what's real?** (epistemology)
- **How does the real arise from the material?** (metaphysics of layered reality)
- **How do real systems behave over time?** (dynamics)

One name can cover two of these cleanly ("constitutive realism" covers the first; "constitutive convergence" covers the second). No single name covers all four without diluting the terms beyond usefulness. So the structure is four trunks, each with its own mechanism and its own name.

This is a feature, not a failure. The trunks are **division of labor**, not competitors — each answers a different question about the same reality. They make different claims, carry different load, and are falsifiable on different grounds. A unified philosophy with four precisely-named commitments is stronger than one sprawling label trying to do everything.

## The Four Trunks

### Trunk 1 — Normative Ontology

**Working name:** *Constitutive realism*

**Thesis:** Normative facts (moral, logical, legal) are real, non-arbitrary, and error-admitting because they are **entailed by the structure of the relata**. Morality is constitutive of agency; logic is constitutive of reasoning; legal norms are constitutive of cooperative rule-systems. The facts are mind-dependent (they require agents to exist) but not arbitrary (they are fixed by the structure once the agents exist).

**Mechanism:** Structural entailment. *Given* agents with property and capacity for action, the norm against stealing is generated by the structure of agent-interaction. Same logical form as *"given Peano axioms, 2+2=4."*

**What it contrasts with:** Platonism (no abstract normative realm), constructivism (normative facts aren't arbitrary), classical mind-independent realism (norms don't float in mindless worlds — see the [vacuity test](./morality/constitutive-elective.md#the-vacuity-test--killing-the-mind-independence-move)), anti-realism (convergence and error-correction track something).

### Trunk 2 — Verification Epistemology

**Working name:** *Constitutive convergence*

**Thesis:** Real structures are identified by **independent convergence under reality-pressure**. Independent civilizations converge on core moral norms; independent traditions converge on the same logical insights; independent measurement systems converge on the same physical patterns. Convergence is evidence for the reality of what's being tracked, because agents who aren't communicating have no shared cause for agreement *other than* the structure of what they're both interacting with.

**Mechanism:** Reality-pressured filtering. Agents try, fail, update, accumulate state. Over long enough timescales across enough independent observers, the attempts that track reality compound and the ones that don't collapse. What survives is what reality allows to survive. Error is possible at any instant; convergence is the long-run signal.

**What it contrasts with:** Foundationalism (no infallible ground required), pure coherentism (coherence alone doesn't pick out reality), social-construction views (convergence across non-communicating traditions rules out arbitrary construction), universal skepticism (accumulated state is a genuine verification layer).

### Trunk 3 — Emergence Metaphysics

**Working name:** Unnamed. Candidate territory: *substrate emergentism*, *organized emergence*, *pattern emergentism*, *emergent structuralism*.

**Thesis:** Real immaterial structures arise from organized material substrate. The arithmetic a computer performs is immaterial but real; it emerges from the arrangement of transistors. The same template covers logic from brains, morality from social groups, consciousness from neural networks. The emergent patterns are **substrate-independent** (same arithmetic from different physical realizations) but **substrate-dependent** for their existence (no transistors, no computation).

**Mechanism:** Organization producing pattern. Not reducible to components; not floating above them; arising from their arrangement. The pattern is real because it constrains physical systems and makes correct predictions. It isn't magical because the mechanism is visible at every level (water from H₂O, computation from transistors, logic from cognition).

**What it contrasts with:** Platonism (patterns don't exist in an abstract realm), eliminative materialism (the pattern is real, not just a way of talking), dualism (no separate substance), theism-as-ground (God isn't required to explain how immaterial patterns arise from organized matter).

### Trunk 4 — Structural Dynamics

**Working name:** Unnamed. Candidate territory: *opposing-forces structure*, *irreducibility dynamics*, *gradient structure*, *structural tension*.

**Thesis:** Real structured systems exhibit predictable dynamics over time. Every concentration of power generates counter-forces. Every governing system faces Gödel-shaped completeness/consistency walls. Every attempt to organize computationally irreducible agents generates unintended responses. Every civilizational arc follows recognizable rise-and-fall patterns. These aren't coincidences of unrelated phenomena — they're the same structural principle at different scales.

**Mechanism:** Candidates are named but not settled: computational irreducibility, thermodynamic equilibrium-seeking, game-theoretic inevitability, Gödel-style incompleteness at governance scale. The trunk's task is to work out which mechanisms ground which dynamics.

**What it contrasts with:** Static structural views (structures don't sit still), unique-cause explanations (the pattern appears in too many domains to have a single domain-specific cause), and linear-progress views (the dynamics include regression, cycles, and collapse).

## How the Trunks Connect

The trunks aren't independent silos. They divide the work:

- **Trunk 1 ↔ Trunk 4 — the load-bearing partnership.** Trunk 1 answers *what holds, given conditions*. Trunk 4 answers *how those conditions persist, shift, and are discovered*. The apparent stability of moral facts comes from the stability of Trunk 4 conditions — humans have to eat, cooperate, die — so the norms downstream stay load-bearing across centuries. When conditions shift (contraception, AI, abundance), the facts update, but the constitutive *method of generation* stays the same. Stability and change are not in opposition; they're the same process at different timescales.
- **Trunk 1 needs Trunk 2.** Constitutive facts are real, but that's an empty claim without a method for identifying them. Convergence under reality-pressure is that method. Trunk 2 is what makes Trunk 1 more than assertion.
- **Trunk 1 needs Trunk 3.** Constitutive facts are real *and* mind-dependent. That combination is coherent only if immaterial real patterns can arise from organized matter. Emergence supplies the metaphysical room for Trunk 1 to breathe.
- **Trunk 3 needs Trunk 4.** Emergence explains how real patterns arise; dynamics explains how they behave once present. Without dynamics, emergence describes a static layer cake. With it, emergence is a living process with characteristic behaviors at scale.
- **Trunk 2 polices all three.** Convergence is the filter that tests whether any claim in any trunk is tracking reality or just sounding good. It's the shared verification method.

Four trunks, five joints. That's the shape.

## Naming Method

Sub-names settle first; the umbrella name settles last. This is deliberate:

- Naming the umbrella first produces a word that either misses a trunk (too narrow) or covers nothing specific (too vague).
- Naming each trunk first forces each concept to declare what it actually claims. The umbrella then has to be what the four names jointly point at.
- The sub-names should each carry a *mechanism word* (what it claims structurally) and a *stance word* (what epistemic status it asserts). "Constitutive realism" = mechanism + stance. "Constitutive convergence" = mechanism + method. Trunks 3 and 4 should follow the same shape when named.

The umbrella name, when it comes, will probably include: *emergence* (the metaphysical commitment), *structure* (the common thread across all four), and something that signals the verification orientation (convergence, accumulation, or similar). Candidates to sit with: *emergent structural realism*, *convergent emergentism*, *accumulated structural realism*. None have survived the friction test yet.

## The Umbrella Thesis

Even without a settled umbrella name, the joint claim can be stated:

> *Reality has structure. Structure emerges from organized substrate. What's real about a domain is identified by independent convergence under reality-pressure. Real structured systems exhibit characteristic dynamics over time.*

Four sentences, four trunks, one philosophy.
