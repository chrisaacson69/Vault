# The Stance-Independence Retreat — A Rename, Not a Repair
> When moral realists abandon "mind-independent" and retreat to "stance-independent," they're not solving the vocabulary problem — they're hiding it. The term slides between two incompatible readings, one of which is a concession and the other of which inherits every defect of mind-independence. This page names the move, disambiguates the readings, and supplies the attack set.

**Links:** [The Four Trunks](../the-four-trunks.md), [Relational Objectivity](./relational-objectivity.md), [Epistemology](./README.md), [The Constitutive/Elective Distinction](../morality/constitutive-elective.md), [Morality — Debate FAQ](../morality/debate-faq.md), [Wilson vs Objectivism: Stance Dependence](../../debates/wilson-objectivism-stance-dependence.md), [Wilson on Triggernometry](../../debates/wilson-triggernometry-christian-nationalism.md), [Noerr vs Terry X](../../debates/noerr-teryx-subjective-objective-morality.md)

**Trunk:** This page defends [Trunk 1 — Normative Ontology](../the-four-trunks.md#trunk-1--normative-ontology) by dissecting a common evasion move; it uses [Trunk 2 — Verification Epistemology](../the-four-trunks.md#trunk-2--verification-epistemology) (the convergence test) as one of its attack tools.

## The Problem

The [constitutive/elective page](../morality/constitutive-elective.md) diagnoses why "objective" and "subjective" are broken: each does double duty, collapsing two distinct axes into one word. The vacuity test retires strict mind-independence as incoherent. Savvy realists have noticed.

The result is a rhetorical migration. Instead of "moral facts are *objective* / *mind-independent*," the defended claim becomes "moral facts are *stance-independent* — true regardless of what anyone thinks about them." We see this from Andrew Wilson (Triggernometry, and earlier debates), from streamers discussing "moral worth" as a moral fact, and increasingly in popular metaethics. The move sounds modest: it doesn't demand a mindless moral realm, only that individual attitudes don't settle truth.

The problem: "stance-independent" is ambiguous between two incompatible readings. Left unsaid, the ambiguity does all the rhetorical work — the realist defends the weak reading and cashes in on the strong one. Once disambiguated, one reading is a concession into the constitutive framework and the other inherits every defect of mind-independence.

## Two Readings of "Stance-Independent"

**Reading A — Attitude-independent:**
The truth of a moral claim doesn't depend on any *particular* agent's stance toward it. Even if every agent disagreed, the fact could still hold (or fail to hold). This is compatible with morality being mind-*dependent* at the ontological level — it just rules out the specific dependency in which individual opinion determines truth.

**Reading B — Agent-independent:**
The truth of a moral claim doesn't depend on the existence of any agents at all. Would hold "regardless of circumstances," including circumstances in which no minds exist. This is mind-independence repackaged.

These are not minor variants. They live on different axes:

| Reading | Compatible with mind-dependence? | Survives vacuity test? | Position it collapses into |
|---------|-----------------------------------|------------------------|-----------------------------|
| A — Attitude-independent | Yes | Yes (doesn't take mindless states as its truth-makers) | Constitutive realism |
| B — Agent-independent | No | No (vacuous over mindless worlds) | Classical mind-independent realism |

The realist typically asserts (A)-strength and defends (B)-strength consequences, or vice versa. Pin them to one and the other retreat closes.

## Why the Retreat Happens

The vacuity test kills (B). *"Strip the minds out of the universe — what does 'stealing is wrong' refer to?"* Nothing. Same for "all humans have equal moral worth" (no humans, no worth, no referent). The realist noticed this failure — or has been shown it — and needed a term that:

1. Preserves the non-arbitrariness intuition "objective" used to carry
2. Doesn't obviously require a mindless moral realm
3. Still blocks the interlocutor from saying "well that's just your opinion"

"Stance-independent" does all three — *as long as the ambiguity is preserved*. Once forced to pick (A) or (B), one answer costs them the position and the other reopens the vacuity attack.

## The Attack Set

### 1. The disambiguation demand

The core move. Force the speaker to specify which reading they're defending.

> *"When you say stance-independent, do you mean (a) it doesn't depend on any individual's attitude — even though it does depend on the existence of agents — or (b) it would hold even in a universe with no agents at all?"*

- If (a): they've conceded the mind-dependence axis. Morality requires minds; some moral facts are just non-arbitrary given that minds exist. Welcome them to [constitutive realism](../morality/constitutive-elective.md). The debate is now about the *source* of the constitutive relationship (agency, selection, design), not whether stance-independence is a kind of mind-independence.
- If (b): every move from [the vacuity test section](../morality/constitutive-elective.md#the-vacuity-test--killing-the-mind-independence-move) applies unchanged. Strip minds out, ask what the fact refers to, watch the three retreat moves collapse as before.

There is no stable third reading. The vocabulary doesn't mark a new metaethical position — it marks *ambiguity between two old ones*.

### 2. The grounding-regress demand

"Stance-independent" describes a *property* (independence from attitudes); it doesn't specify the *ground*. Ask what makes the fact stance-independent. Exactly three answers exist, and all three are already mapped:

- **"It just is" / self-evidence** — axiomatic stop. Equivalent to the old "objective means it just is." No epistemic progress, just a word substitution.
- **"The structure of agency / reality entails it"** — concedes into the constitutive framework. The fact is stance-independent *because* agency has a structure that generates it. That's the vault's position under a different label.
- **"God grounds it"** — concedes mind-dependence on a specific necessary mind (Move 1 from the vacuity page). Stance-independence is now truth-relative-to-God's-mind. The ontology axis is lost; only the theology remains.

No fourth answer is available. Each collapse lands the speaker somewhere already analyzed.

### 3. The "regardless of circumstances" slide

Speakers regularly phrase stance-independence as "true *regardless of circumstances*." This slides from Reading A into Reading B without announcing the move. "Circumstances" quietly includes *whether agents exist*, which is precisely what Reading A is not supposed to cover.

Call it out:

> *"'Regardless of circumstances' — does that include a universe with no agents? If yes, you've reclaimed mind-independence and we're back at the vacuity test. If no, then you only mean regardless of current attitudes, which is weaker than the way you're using the claim."*

This is the exact step the streamer took with "all humans have equal moral worth." The word "circumstances" does the smuggling.

### 4. The functional parody

If "stance-independent" means "true regardless of any agent's opinion," the label attaches equally to any contested claim the speaker disagrees with. *"The Pope is the vicar of Christ"* is stance-independently true for Catholics and stance-independently false for Protestants. Both parties have equal formal right to the label — its application settles nothing.

Stance-independence, asserted rather than grounded, is an empty stamp. It asserts a *modal status* (the claim is objective-ish) without supplying the *structural fact* that would make the modal status non-trivial. The constitutive framework supplies the structural fact (the norm is entailed by agent-interaction). Bare stance-independence doesn't.

### 5. The convergence test on the specific claim

The vault's diagnostic for *constitutive necessity* is independent convergence across cultures and conditions ([relational objectivity](./relational-objectivity.md), [constitutive-elective](../morality/constitutive-elective.md)). Apply it to whatever the speaker offers as their stance-independent fact.

- "Don't murder" — meets convergence. Strong candidate.
- "Don't steal" — meets convergence with variations in property definitions.
- "All humans have equal moral worth" — fails convergence badly. Hierarchical societies dominate the historical record; strict moral equality is a very recent and geographically narrow norm. If stance-independence is compatible with near-universal historical rejection, it's epistemically inert — you can't use it to settle disputes, because everyone who disagrees can claim the same label.

This forces a dilemma:
- Accept that stance-independence doesn't track convergence → the label has no diagnostic power
- Reject the claim as stance-independent → you've walked back the move

### 6. The operational pressure

Even granting stance-independence, the specific claims offered are underspecified. Press "all humans have equal moral worth" for operational content:

- "Treated identically under law" — political/contractual claim, not metaphysical. Stance-independence does no work; the argument is institutional.
- "Identical intrinsic value in some cosmic register" — what register? Show the evidence. Without a register, this is a feeling in search of a referent.
- "The structure of agent-interaction entails reciprocal respect" — now we're in constitutive territory, and *this is a contested empirical-structural claim*. Hoppe's argumentation ethics gives self-ownership but not strict equality. Aristotelian frameworks give hierarchy of virtue. The constitutive case for strict equality is live, not settled.

"Stance-independent" papers over this. The realist gets the authority of metaphysics without paying for the specification.

## Worked Example — "All Humans Have Equal Moral Worth"

A real case in the wild: a classical-liberal streamer with a legal background offered this as a "stance-independent moral fact — true regardless of circumstances." Running the attack set on it shows how the disambiguation demand, the scope split, and the convergence test close the move.

### The claim at legal scope — defensible, but not a moral fact

At the legal layer the principle does real work: equal standing under law, equal protection, one-person-one-vote, equal treatment in court. A classical liberal defending this has good ground. But the ground is **institutional-consequentialist**, not metaphysical:

- Nobody trusts the state to correctly rank individuals by worth
- The epistemic problem of weighting people is intractable at scale
- Procedural equality is a Schelling point that resists corruption
- Populations that adopt it (paired with the right scope conditions) outperform populations that don't

That is a [deontology-as-cached-consequentialism](../morality/deontology-consequentialism.md) argument for a legal rule. It is "good standard" — same epistemic status as "representative government is a good institutional arrangement" or "property rights produce better outcomes than their absence." None of those are moral facts. They're defeasible institutional choices with empirical backing.

And even at the legal layer, the principle is openly defeasible:

- Criminals forfeit rights through their own actions (due process *exists* to produce legitimate legal asymmetry)
- Minors have different standing than adults
- Non-citizens have different standing than citizens
- Combatants can be killed; mentally incompetent adults have different standing
- Parents have legal priority over their own children vs. strangers' children

If "equal moral worth" were actually stance-independent in a robust metaphysical sense, every one of these asymmetries would require extraordinary justification. In practice they're treated as obvious. The principle works because it's scoped, not because it's universal.

### The claim at personal scope — empirically false

At the personal-moral scope the claim collapses. The cleanest counterexample is the **burning-embryos case** (a variant of the classic embryo rescue case used against strong pro-life metaphysics): a building is about to collapse, one side has a newborn, the other has 100 frozen embryos, you can save only one side. Everyone saves the newborn — including committed pro-life advocates. If 100 embryos actually had equal moral worth to a newborn, the utilitarian answer is to save the 100. Nobody chooses that. Empirical behavior reveals the actual weighting, which is not equal.

Extend the case in either direction and it gets worse:

- Your child vs. a stranger's child — you save yours, and everyone (including the stranger) would judge you a moral monster for doing otherwise
- Your spouse vs. a random stranger — same
- Your parent vs. a random stranger — same
- Your countryman vs. a foreign citizen in wartime — same
- A great scientist vs. a convicted serial killer — even committed egalitarians hesitate

This isn't a marginal bias to overcome. It's the **core structure of actual moral psychology** (kin selection in evolutionary biology, Haidt's moral foundations research, every religious tradition's treatment of filial duty, universal across cultures with no exceptions). Strict equal moral worth at personal scope isn't a higher morality we fail to reach — it's a claim that gets the structure of morality backward.

### The convergence test trashes the universal reading

Apply the [relational-objectivity](./relational-objectivity.md) diagnostic — independent convergence across cultures and conditions — to strict equal moral worth:

- Hierarchical societies dominate the historical record
- Kin preference is culturally universal (no exceptions)
- Every modern liberal democracy institutionalizes asymmetries (citizenship, minority, criminality)
- "Equal moral worth" as an articulated norm is narrow in time (~250 years of sustained articulation) and narrow in geography (Western liberal democracies + influenced regions)

What actually converges across conditions is something weaker and more specific: *procedural equal standing under a common rule-system among agents with reciprocal buy-in*. That is a scope-bounded, contractual norm. It is not a universal metaphysical fact about humans.

### The scope-confusion diagnosis

The claim is a [scope confusion](../morality/scope-confusion.md) wearing metaethical clothing:

| Scope | Reading | Status |
|-------|---------|--------|
| **Law scope** | Equal procedural standing under shared rules | Defensible, institutional, consequentialist, defeasible — "good standard" |
| **Morality scope** | Equal moral weight of all humans regardless of relationship | Empirically false, universally violated, structurally required to be violated |
| **Metaethics** | Stance-independent moral fact | Borrows authority from both, defends neither |

The move's plausibility depends on the listener hearing the legal-scope reading (which is defensible) while the speaker assertively claims the metaethical reading (which isn't). Pin it to one scope and the claim either reduces to a good institutional principle or becomes empirically false.

### The direct challenge

> *"Do you mean that equal standing under law is a good institutional arrangement — which I'd agree with, and which is an empirical-consequentialist claim, not a metaphysical one — or do you mean you'd be morally obligated to let your own child drown to save two strangers' children? Because the first is defensible on outcome grounds and the second is false to every moral tradition in history, including the one you're drawing from."*

The speaker will retreat to the first. At that point the claim isn't a moral fact; it's a legal design principle with empirical backing. "Legal design principle with empirical backing" is not stance-independent in any sense that distinguishes it from a hundred other institutional choices.

### Why the constitutive framework handles this better

The [constitutive/elective framework](../morality/constitutive-elective.md) has no trouble with any of this, because it never needed a universal-metaphysical-fact layer to do the work:

- **Legal-scope equal standing** is analyzed as a *constitutive norm of cooperative rule-systems among reciprocal agents* — structurally entailed once agents have buy-in to a shared legal frame, empirically validated by outcome data, defeasible when the buy-in conditions fail (criminality, hostility, incompetence). This is a scope-bounded constitutive claim with a specified mechanism.
- **Personal-scope differential weighting** is analyzed as the actual shape of agent morality — kin preference, reciprocity, locality, contract — structurally entailed by how cooperation is built across nested groups. The scope gradient from the scope-confusion page already captures it.
- **The apparent tension** between "equal under law" and "differential in personal life" is not a tension at all once you have scope layers. Both are constitutive within their scope; neither has to be universal; neither is stance-independent in a metaethically loaded sense.

The stance-independence move tries to collapse these into a single universal fact. The constitutive framework doesn't need to, and doesn't. That is the payoff of splitting the axes honestly instead of hiding them behind a relabeled term.

## What the Move Is Actually Doing

A clean description:

1. The realist noticed that "mind-independent" fails the vacuity test.
2. They adopted a weaker-sounding term — "stance-independent" — that is ambiguous between a concession (A) and the original failing position (B).
3. They defend at (A)-strength (hard to argue against) and cash in at (B)-strength (does the original realist work).
4. The ambiguity is the argument. Remove it and the position either reduces to the vault's framework or reinstates a failed one.

This is not a new metaethical view. It is the objective/subjective confusion, compressed into a single word and redeployed. The [constitutive/elective distinction](../morality/constitutive-elective.md) is the honest repair of the same problem — splitting axes explicitly, naming the missing cell, specifying the mechanism, supplying a test. Stance-independence tries to achieve the same repair covertly, without paying any of the structural costs.

## Where This Leaves the Realist

Stripped of the ambiguity, the realist has the same options the vacuity page already laid out:

- **Reading A → constitutive realism** — the real live debate about what grounds the constitutive relationship (agency, selection, design). Productive, open.
- **Reading B → classical mind-independence** — already retired by vacuity. Any defense requires the three retreat moves and each lands in a known basin.
- **Neither → give up the realist label** — acknowledge that what's being defended is non-arbitrariness, not stance-independence in any metaphysically loaded sense.

The last option is the honest exit. "Non-arbitrary moral claims grounded in the structure of agency" is a position worth defending. "Stance-independent moral facts" tries to defend it while also claiming something stronger — and the stronger part doesn't survive scrutiny.

## Connections to the Wilson Cluster

Wilson runs stance-*dependence* as a weapon against progressives (they can't ground morality because their frame is stance-dependent) while implicitly claiming stance-*independence* for Christian morality. Both moves treat stance-(in)dependence as a foundational property rather than as a derived feature of the structure of agency. Wilson's own position has the same problem he weaponizes against his opponents — this is catalogued in [Wilson vs Objectivism: Stance Dependence](../../debates/wilson-objectivism-stance-dependence.md).

The symmetry is diagnostic: if stance-(in)dependence were a coherent metaethical category, one side of a debate would be able to use it to defeat the other. In practice, each side can deploy it with equal formal right, and neither deployment settles anything. That is the signature of a label doing no work.

## Open Questions

1. **Is there a defensible (A)-only reading of "stance-independent" that doesn't collapse into the constitutive claim?** Candidate: "true across all actual agents' attitudes but not necessarily grounded in agency-structure." This is closer to "universal de facto agreement," which is weaker than realism and empirically brittle.
2. **Do any metaethicists defend (B) directly post-vacuity?** Shafer-Landau's non-naturalism is closest, but leans on sui generis moral properties that face the same dragons move the vacuity page already addresses.
3. **Can the stance-independence move be rehabilitated by coupling it with a specific constitutive mechanism?** Yes — and that's the vault's position under a different name. The question is whether the realist will pay that price publicly.
