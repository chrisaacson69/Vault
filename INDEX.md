    - [Gödel’s Incompleteness Theorems](./research/philosophy/logic-and-math/godel-incompleteness.md) — no powerful formal system is both complete and consistent, nor can it prove its own consistency; self-reference as the common mechanism; the limits are a signature of emergence, not a flaw

    - [Quantum Logic](./research/philosophy/logic-and-math/quantum-logic.md) — the one non-classical logic nature demanded rather than philosophers invented; the distributive law is experimentally falsified, so logic is empirical and revisable — and TAG falls with it
    - [The Translation Problem](./research/philosophy/logic-and-math/the-translation-problem.md) — formalizing reality into propositions is interpretive and pre-logical; ambiguity, scope, and the material conditional’s indifference; most debates are translation failures, not logic failures
